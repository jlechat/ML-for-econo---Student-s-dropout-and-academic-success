## PICO - Population, Intervention, Comparison, Outcome framework for our study 

The table below summarizes the framework our study is based on. 
|Component      | Description                                            | 
|:-------------:|:-------------------------------------------------------|
|Population     |The population of our study is coposed of higher eductaion students from Portugal. The data contains social and demographic information about students. It also contains the information on their academic path, whether they were granted a scolarship and the application order (was the programme their first choice?)|
|Intervetion    | We want to study two different intervention effects. First of all, studying whether getting a scolarship has an effect on dropout. Second intervention we would like to study is the effect of getting in one of the top-choice (for ex, top 3 choices) on dropout. | 
|Control        | For the first study, it would be interesting to compare students to a control group who have not recieved a scolarship but have similar characteristics (study area, socio-demographics). For the second study, the idea would be to compare students who got one of their top-3 choices to those who got into their 4th+ favorite programme. |   
|Outcome        | The outcome of interest is the variable that has 3 modalities (classification task) - enrolled, graduate and dropout. We would like to see the impact of our two chosen variables on dropout rate or the probability of dropout (binarizing the outcome variable) | 

### Hypotheses : 
Following the PICO analysis, we formulate the two following hypotheses : 
- We expect the effect of getting a scolarship to be negative on the probability of dropout.
- We expect the effect of getting in one of the least favorite higher education institutions to be positive on the dropout rate or probability.
